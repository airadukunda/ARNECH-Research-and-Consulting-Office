Our Mission
Why ARNECH Exists

ARNECH Research and Consulting advances knowledge and drives innovation by providing research services and strategic consulting to organizations, institutions, and governments. We bridge the gap between academic research and practical implementation across Africa and beyond.

Founded by a team of researchers with expertise spanning medicine, statistics, economics, and data science, ARNECH brings rigorous academic standards to real-world challenges.

Our interdisciplinary approach ensures that research findings translate into actionable policies and programs that improve lives — from reducing disease burden to strengthening financial inclusion and building community resilience.

Our Vision

To be a leading research and consulting organisation that transforms evidence into action for sustainable development and improved health outcomes in Africa and globally.

Home / Services
Services

Research & Consulting
Solutions

From study design to policy implementation — comprehensive support across the research and consulting lifecycle.

Research Design & Implementation

We design and execute comprehensive research studies using cutting-edge methodologies. Our interdisciplinary approach ensures robust findings that inform policy and practice across health, education, and social sciences. We cover qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods designs.

Strategic Consulting

Expert consultants provide strategic guidance to organisations seeking to improve their projects, implement evidence-based practices, and navigate complex challenges. We support NGOs, governments, international agencies, and private sector actors.

Data Analytics & Insights

We transform complex data into actionable insights using advanced analytical techniques — statistical modelling, machine learning, and data visualisation — helping clients make informed decisions grounded in solid evidence.

Policy Analysis & Development

We analyse existing policies and develop new frameworks addressing contemporary challenges. Our expertise spans public health, education, and social policy, with a special focus on low- and middle-income country contexts.

Capacity Building & Training

Customised training programs, workshops, and mentorship initiatives that strengthen local capabilities and ensure sustainable impact. We design bespoke curricula aligned to organisational needs and local contexts.

Grant Writing & Funding Support

Our experienced team assists organisations in securing funding through compelling grant proposals and funding strategies. We have a proven track record of successful applications to major international funding agencies.

Monitoring & Evaluation

End-to-end M&E framework design, implementation, and reporting. We build systems for routine data collection, indicator tracking, and programme performance assessment aligned to donor and government requirements.

Disease Modelling & Forecasting

Mathematical and statistical models for infectious disease dynamics, outbreak analysis, and epidemic forecasting. We provide scenario-based analyses to support public health decision-making and resource allocation.

Digital Health Solutions

Development of health information systems, digital tools, and innovative technologies for improving healthcare delivery in resource-limited settings, including mobile health and data management platforms.

Research Unit

Modelling &
Simulation Unit

Mathematical modelling and epidemiological analysis to forecast disease trajectories and inform public health responses.

Mathematical ModellingEpidemic ForecastingTB · Malaria · MeaslesOutbreak Analysis

The Modelling & Simulation Unit applies cutting-edge mathematical and statistical methods to understand and predict the behaviour of infectious diseases in human populations. By developing compartmental models (SIR, SEIR, and their extensions), the unit provides scenario-based analyses that directly inform public health planning, resource allocation, and policy development.

Led by Dr. Arnaud Iradukunda, whose Oxford MSc focused specifically on modelling for global health, the unit has active projects on tuberculosis (including the impact of COVID-19 on TB dynamics and the potential of the M72/AS01E vaccine), malaria elimination strategy modelling, and broader epidemiological surveillance work.

Core Capabilities

  • Compartmental modelling (SIR/SEIR/SEIRD) for infectious disease dynamics
  • Scenario analysis and counterfactual modelling for intervention planning
  • Real-time outbreak detection and epidemic curve analysis
  • Drug resistance modelling for TB and antimicrobial resistance
  • Epidemiological study design (cohort, cross-sectional, case-control)
  • Global burden of disease analysis and Lancet GBD collaboration
  • Spatial epidemiology and geographic information systems (GIS)

Active Research Areas

  • COVID-19 impact on tuberculosis mortality and drug resistance in Burundi (Harvard University partnership, 2023–2025)
  • Mathematical modelling for malaria control and elimination (2027 and 2030 targets)
  • M72/AS01E vaccine impact on tuberculosis burden — scenario analysis

The Vaccine Research & Evaluation Unit is dedicated to producing rigorous evidence on the effectiveness, impact, and optimal deployment of vaccines in sub-Saharan Africa. Our Contribution to the
Sustainable Development Goals

ARNECH's research, consulting, and capacity-building work directly advances five UN Sustainable Development Goals across health, economics, education, and equity in Africa and beyond.

3
SDG 3

Good Health & Well-Being

Good Health & Well-Being is the core of ARNECH's scientific mission. Through epidemiological research, mathematical modelling, and evidence synthesis, we generate knowledge that directly informs health policy and programme design in Burundi, East Africa, and globally.

Tuberculosis Research & COVID-19 Impact

ARNECH led a case-control study on TB mortality and multidrug resistance before and during COVID-19 in Burundi (published in BMC Infectious Diseases, 2025), generating critical evidence for national TB programme recovery. The team also modelled the impact of the M72/AS01E TB vaccine candidate, providing scenario-based projections under different coverage assumptions to support national immunisation planning.

Mathematical Modelling for Malaria Elimination

ARNECH is developing mathematical models to inform Burundi's malaria control and elimination strategy aligned to the 2027 and 2030 national targets. These dynamic transmission models incorporate spatio-temporal heterogeneity and intervention coverage to support resource allocation decisions by the Ministry of Health.

Non-Communicable Diseases & Burden of Disease

Team members have contributed to landmark Global Burden of Disease studies in The Lancet covering hypertension, stroke, diabetes, and antimicrobial resistance. ARNECH's own research established prevalence rates and risk factors for hypertension in Burundi (PLOS ONE, 2021) and profiled NCD burden for national planning.

Maternal & Newborn Health

Research on neonatal survival and determinants of newborn mortality in Burundi (2024), antenatal care spatial distribution and predictors (2023), and transfusion-transmitted infections in blood donors (2024) directly supports maternal and child health programme design in partnership with the Burundi Ministry of Health and ABUBEF.

Neglected Tropical Diseases

ARNECH conducted a spatio-temporal analysis of community-directed ivermectin treatment coverage in Burundi in support of the WHO 2030 NTD roadmap, identifying geographic gaps and informing programme targeting strategies.

1
SDG 1

No Poverty

ARNECH's Economic & Social Business Unit, led by Gildas Irakoze, examines the structural drivers of poverty in Burundi and the Great Lakes region, with a focus on financial systems, gender equity, and inclusive economic design.

Financial Inclusion & Mobile Money

Funded by the African Economic Research Consortium (2024–2025), ARNECH is examining mobile money's role in financial inclusion with gender and location-disaggregated perspectives. An earlier study (Research Square, 2022) explored financial inclusion and gender patterns in poverty reduction in Burundi, contributing to evidence-based financial sector reform.

Infant & Child Nutrition Economics

In partnership with international researchers, ARNECH contributed to difference-in-difference evaluations of integrated nutrition interventions and baseline assessments of infant and child health status (BMC Nutrition, 2020), linking economic vulnerability to early childhood outcomes.

Agricultural Value Chains & Rural Economics

ARNECH applies cooperative and social solidarity economy models to support smallholder farmers, women's savings groups, and rural cooperatives in Burundi — translating economic research into practical tools for poverty reduction.

Reduced Inequalities

Addressing inequality — whether in health access, financial opportunity, or educational resources — is a thread running through all of ARNECH's work. We apply disaggregated analysis by gender, geography, income, and ethnicity to expose and address structural disparities.

Health Equity Research

Research on antenatal care coverage in Burundi (2023) used spatial analysis to identify underserved communities and predictors of non-utilisation. Gender-Disaggregated Analysis
