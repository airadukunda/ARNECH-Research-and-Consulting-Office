Our Mission
Why ARNECH Exists

ARNECH Research and Consulting advances knowledge and drives innovation by providing research services and strategic consulting to organizations, institutions, and governments. We bridge the gap between academic research and practical implementation across Africa and beyond.

Founded by a team of researchers with expertise spanning medicine, statistics, economics, and data science, ARNECH brings rigorous academic standards to real-world challenges.

Our interdisciplinary approach ensures that research findings translate into actionable policies and programs that improve lives — from reducing disease burden to strengthening financial inclusion and building community resilience.

Our Vision

To be a leading research and consulting organisation that transforms evidence into action for sustainable development and improved health outcomes in Africa and globally.

Services

Research & Consulting
Solutions

From study design to policy implementation — comprehensive support across the research and consulting lifecycle.

Research Design & Implementation

We design and execute comprehensive research studies using cutting-edge methodologies. Our interdisciplinary approach ensures robust findings that inform policy and practice across health, education, and social sciences. We cover qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods designs.

Strategic Consulting

Expert consultants provide strategic guidance to organisations seeking to improve their projects, implement evidence-based practices, and navigate complex challenges. We support NGOs, governments, international agencies, and private sector actors.

Data Analytics & Insights

We transform complex data into actionable insights using advanced analytical techniques — statistical modelling, machine learning, and data visualisation — helping clients make informed decisions grounded in solid evidence.

Policy Analysis & Development

We analyse existing policies and develop new frameworks addressing contemporary challenges. Our expertise spans public health, education, and social policy, with a special focus on low- and middle-income country contexts.

Capacity Building & Training

Customised training programs, workshops, and mentorship initiatives that strengthen local capabilities and ensure sustainable impact. We design bespoke curricula aligned to organisational needs and local contexts.

Grant Writing & Funding Support

Our experienced team assists organisations in securing funding through compelling grant proposals and funding strategies. We have a proven track record of successful applications to major international funding agencies.

Research Unit

Monitoring &
Evaluation Unit

Designing and implementing rigorous M&E systems that transform programme data into evidence for learning and decision-making.

M&E FrameworksImpact EvaluationUSAID / WHO StandardsData Systems

The Monitoring & Evaluation Unit is one of ARNECH's most established divisions, drawing on deep practical experience from major USAID-funded health programmes in Burundi and the wider East African region. The unit specialises in designing fit-for-purpose M&E systems that generate reliable evidence to guide programme management, donor reporting, and policy influence.

Led by Nestor Ntakaburimvo, whose career has spanned MEL roles at ICAP Columbia University and Pathfinder International, the unit brings a practitioner's understanding of what M&E looks like under real-world conditions — balancing rigour with feasibility in resource-constrained environments.

Core Capabilities

  • M&E framework and results framework development aligned to logic models and theories of change
  • Indicator selection, definition, and data quality assurance systems
  • Baseline, midline, and endline evaluation design and implementation
  • DHIS2 and REDCap database design and management
  • Qualitative process evaluations and most significant change studies
  • Routine data quality assessments (RDQA) and data verification exercises
  • Capacity building for national M&E teams and local implementing partners
  • Performance-based financing and results-based management frameworks

Sectors Covered

  • HIV/AIDS care and treatment (PEPFAR / USAID aligned)
  • Sexual and reproductive health and family planning
  • Maternal, newborn, and child health
  • Nutrition and food security
  • Education and vocational training
  • Agriculture and rural livelihoods

Home / Units / Disease Modelling & Epidemiology
Research Unit

Modelling &
Simulation Unit

Mathematical modelling and epidemiological analysis to forecast disease trajectories and inform public health responses.

Mathematical ModellingEpidemic ForecastingTB · Malaria · MeaslesOutbreak Analysis

The Modelling & Simulation Unit applies cutting-edge mathematical and statistical methods to understand and predict the behaviour of infectious diseases in human populations. By developing compartmental models (SIR, SEIR, and their extensions), the unit provides scenario-based analyses that directly inform public health planning, resource allocation, and policy development.

Led by Dr. Arnaud Iradukunda, whose Oxford MSc focused specifically on modelling for global health, the unit has active projects on tuberculosis (including the impact of COVID-19 on TB dynamics and the potential of the M72/AS01E vaccine), malaria elimination strategy modelling, and broader epidemiological surveillance work.

Core Capabilities

  • Compartmental modelling (SIR/SEIR/SEIRD) for infectious disease dynamics
  • Scenario analysis and counterfactual modelling for intervention planning
  • Real-time outbreak detection and epidemic curve analysis
  • Drug resistance modelling for TB and antimicrobial resistance
  • Epidemiological study design (cohort, cross-sectional, case-control)
  • Global burden of disease analysis and Lancet GBD collaboration
  • Spatial epidemiology and geographic information systems (GIS)

Active Research Areas

  • COVID-19 impact on tuberculosis mortality and drug resistance in Burundi (Harvard University partnership, 2023–2025)
  • Mathematical modelling for malaria control and elimination (2027 and 2030 targets)
  • M72/AS01E vaccine impact on tuberculosis burden — scenario analysis

Home / Units / Vaccine Research & Evaluation
Research Unit

Vaccine Research &
Evaluation Unit

Generating evidence on vaccine effectiveness and immunisation strategies to accelerate disease control and elimination.

Vaccine EffectivenessImmunisation StrategyDisease EliminationPolicy Modelling

The Vaccine Research & Evaluation Unit is dedicated to producing rigorous evidence on the effectiveness, impact, and optimal deployment of vaccines in sub-Saharan Africa. Working at the intersection of epidemiology, health economics, and mathematical modelling, the unit evaluates both existing immunisation programmes and emerging vaccine candidates.

With infectious diseases like tuberculosis, malaria, and measles imposing enormous burdens on Burundi and the wider region, this unit's work directly contributes to national and regional strategies for disease control and long-term elimination.

Core capabilities

  • Vaccine effectiveness studies using observational and trial data
  • Modelling optimal vaccination coverage thresholds and strategies
  • Immunisation programme evaluation and cold chain performance assessment
  • Health economic modelling of vaccine introduction and cost-effectiveness
  • Scenario modelling of vaccine impact under different coverage and efficacy assumptions
  • Policy briefs and technical reports for national immunisation programmes

Current focus

  • Mathematical modelling of M72/AS01E tuberculosis vaccine impact in Burundi
  • Measles vaccination coverage analysis and outbreak risk modelling

Home / Units / Economic & Social Business
Research Unit

Economic &
Social Business Unit

Analysing economic systems, building social enterprises, and developing sustainable financing strategies for development impact.

Economic AnalysisFinancial InclusionSocial EnterpriseImpact Assessment

The Economic & Social Business Unit, led by Gildas Irakoze, focuses on the intersection of economic development, social entrepreneurship, and sustainable financing in sub-Saharan Africa. The unit undertakes rigorous quantitative and qualitative research to understand how economic systems function for (and against) marginalised communities, and develops practical models to improve financial inclusion and economic resilience.

With a particular focus on Burundi and the Great Lakes region, the unit's work spans mobile money and digital finance, cooperative economics, agricultural value chains, and poverty analysis — generating evidence that informs national policy and international development programming.

Core Capabilities

  • Financial inclusion research — mobile money, savings groups, microfinance
  • Socio-economic impact assessments for development programmes
  • Agricultural value chain analysis and market systems development
  • Cooperative and social solidarity economy modelling
  • Poverty measurement using multidimensional poverty indices
  • Gender-disaggregated economic analysis and women's economic empowerment
  • Business plan development and social enterprise design for NGOs and cooperatives
  • Cost-benefit and cost-effectiveness analysis for development investments

Active Research

  • Mobile money's role in financial inclusion — gender and location perspectives in Burundi (African Economic Research Consortium, 2024–2025)
  • Greener African cities: remote work and urban economic transition
